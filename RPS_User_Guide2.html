Social Security

Enter the annual benefit you expect to receive from social security. Estimated benefits are found on SSA.GOV. I have found that SSA.GOV estimated benefits assume you will work until your benefit date. So, for the age 70 estimated benefit SSA.GOV assumes you will work to age 70. You will get a more precise estimate by using “ANYPIA32” downloaded from the SSA web site. If you are planning to work until age 62 and delay benefits until age 70, ANYPIA32 will provide a more accurate benefit estimate.

RPS does not support scenarios such as ‘windfall elimination’ rules and ‘file and suspend’.

Roth Conversion

RPS can look for opportunities to do a backdoor Roth Conversion.  If you have enough in your brokerage account to live on for 1-3 years before drawing down on your IRA, it is likely your taxes will be extremely low (if not zero).  This might be a good time to consider a Roth conversion.  RPS will limit the taxes you pay to either the 0%, 10% or 12% tax bracket – depending on your selection.  Try each and see what works best. 

Annual Budget

This should be the amount you minimally want to live on during retirement.  Include non-discretionary expenses such as rent, utilities, health care, groceries.  Also include discretionary expenses such as travel, hobbies and dining out.  In short, enter the amount you plan to spend in a comfortable retirement.  Do not include your mortgage principal and interest in this amount - that is entered in a separate field.   Do not allow for federal income or capital gains taxes – RPS will estimate. 

Annual budget is adjusted by inflation each year.  Mortgage is not.          

RPS separates the budget into 4 different timeframes.  This will allow you to simulate with budgets that will change throughout your retirement.     

RPS includes a method to consider nursing home budget during the last 3 years of your life.  Depending on where you live, nursing home costs could cost at least $100,000 per year.  RPS assumes that if you do want to consider nursing home care costs, it will simulate using those costs the last 3 years of your life.  

Mortgage Amount:

Enter the annual mortgage principal and interest you are paying.  RPS tracks this separately because it is not subject to inflation adjustments.

Enter your age when the last mortgage payment is made.

Stock Allocation %

This is the amount of your retirement assets you feel comfortable investing in stocks.  RPS assumes the remainder will be invested in an intermediate bond index fund. 

Retirement Bonus %:

This is a unique feature of RPS that I have not found with other tools.  RPS will simulate taking a bonus whenever the simulated historical ending balance for a given year is greater than the baseline plan ending balance for the same year. 

For example, assume your baseline plan shows you should have $900,000 for an ending balance after first year of retirement.  Now, let’s say after year 1 of a simulated scenario, the ending balance is $950,000.  If you enter 10% in Retirement Bonus %, RPS will take 10% of the difference ($50,000) and give you a $5000 bonus withdraw for that year.

The idea is for RPS to look for opportunities to withdraw and enjoy your retirement savings if actual results are better than your baseline plan.

Baseline Stock and Bond Returns

The default return rates for the baseline simulation is 6% stock ad 3% for bonds.  You can override this by adjusting these fields.  The bonus paid out during historical simulation will be impacted by your annual baseline ending balance.  

Withdrawals

RPS will start the withdraw simulation after either you or your spouse have retired.

RPS will simulate drawing down your assets in the following sequence: 1) IRA RMD 2) brokerage accounts 3) IRAs non RMD 4) Roth / H.S.A.

RPS assumes withdrawals will occur each year on January 1st.

Annual Process

Inflation adjustments are made starting January 1.

RPS will simulate rebalancing to your preferred asset allocation on January 1 each year.

Roth / HSA

 RPS assumes that H.S.A. and Roth money will be the last accounts to be withdrawn - and all money can be used tax free.   For your H.S.A, the assumption is medical expenses incurred will be greater than the amount withdrawn from your H.S.A. 

Excess RMD

Required minimum distribution from your IRA may exceed your total need (Budget + Taxes – social security and pensions).  In this case, RPS simulation assumes that excess money will be deposited into your brokerage account.

Budget Adjustments When 1 Dies

When testing a scenario where one person dies before the other, RPS will automatically reduce the budget 25%.  It assumed that when one person dies, budget needs are 25% lower. 

Taxes

RPS calculates estimated federal taxes using simple standard deduction assumption. The reason RPS calculates taxes is to help with comparing different retirement scenarios such as the benefits of taking social security at various ages, and benefits of doing a Roth conversation early in retirement.

RPS only assumes 1 (filing single) or 2 exemptions (married filing joint).  No other options are provided.     

Tax implications for employment wages is not considered.  RPS does not consider itemized deductions, alternative minimum tax, or any credits you may receive.  It also does not consider self-employment tax, employment taxes or other taxes that may impact your true final federal tax.

Federal Taxes are calculated and combined in with the following year budget. 

Brokerage account gains are assumed to be long-term and taxed at the capital gains tax rate. 

Calculations for married filing jointly and single filing options are provided.  Head of household and married filed separately are not included.

State income taxes are not calculated.   

Social Security Taxable

 RPS uses the process defined IRS Publication 915 for calculating Social Security Taxable Amount.  Social Security benefit amount will be federally taxable up to 85% of the benefit amount.
